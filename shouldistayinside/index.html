📝 About

Hey there! We are three TUM BSc. students of the Department of Informatics aiming to raise awareness on the importance of staying inside during the COVID-19 pandemic.

As our home state Bavaria enters its fourth week of quarantine in a country badly hit in terms of cases, we’re deeply concerned that the general public may put its guard down when it comes to sheltering in place.

This website allows you to enter the German city you’re located in and simulate how many lives are saved when a social distancing factor is changed. A factor of 0% models what would happen if we as a society didn’t change our behavior at all and still went out to party, eat and mingle. As the factor increases, the disease’s rate of spread gets closer to what was observed in countries such as China, that took rigorous measures to stop the exponential growth of new infections. Note that the exact interpretation of the social distancing factor value is difficult, therefore take our suggestion with a grain of salt. You can however look up the mathematical meaning of the factor on our simulation in the 'Details for nerds' tab.

Our unique approach of showing lives saved instead of lost is an auspicious twist on the grim data usually made available. The data contained here is pulled daily automatically from Fusionbase, a partner of this year’s special HackaTUM edition.

Admittedly, there can be bleak consequences to missing out on real social interactions for a long period of time. For that reason, governments are still allowing people to go out jogging on their own, for instance. Do make sure to get some movement and sunshine in once it a while.

A more in-depth explanation going into the mathematical intricacies of our model is available as a download under the “Details for nerds” tab, where an interactive Matlab SIR Model app can also be found.

We hope the new perspective provided by this tool helps fuel individual change that, when aggregated, results in a behavioral paradigm shift.

So, should I stay home? The insights presented here speak for themselves: as much as possible, since it really makes a difference.

Stay safe!
